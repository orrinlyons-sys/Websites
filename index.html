Notre Histoire

A taste of France in the far north

Nestled beside the Wick River at 2 Market Street, Bord De L'Eau — meaning "edge of the water" — is a hidden gem in the heart of Caithness. Chef-patron Daniel Chrétien brings the soul of authentic French cooking to Scotland's most northerly reaches.

Every dish is crafted with integrity, from hand-selected local seafood fresh from the North Sea, to classic escargots and perfectly seared entrecôte. Daniel's passion for his craft is evident in every plate — and his personal presence in the dining room ensures every guest feels at home.

Bookings are essential. This is a restaurant that rewards those who seek it out.

L'Expérience

What makes us unique

🌊

Riverside Setting

Perched on the banks of the Wick River, our conservatory dining room offers tranquil water views that set the scene for an unhurried meal. The river's presence is felt in the calm and the light.

🥐

Authentically French

Chef-patron Daniel Chrétien is French-trained and personally runs every service. His classical technique and insistence on quality means every dish leaves the kitchen as he intends it.

🦞

North Sea Fresh

We work with the finest Caithness suppliers to source fish and seafood landed just miles away. The North Sea's bounty — lobster, halibut, monkfish — meets the French kitchen at its best.
